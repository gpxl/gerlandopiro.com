If you have no interest in web development or the process of writing and self-publishing, please share this offer with those you think would benefit from it. ### Who the book is for This book is for anyone that has thought about becoming a web developer but hasn't known where to start or what skills they needed to excel in the workplace. Your hoping to land your first job, looking to switch careers, or simply trying to decide if web development is a field you want to pursue. You've probably picked up a little HTML and CSS from online tutorials and maybe even dabbled in a bit of PHP or Ruby. You're excited by what you've learned but the field of Web Development is deep and wide. Your time is precious and you want to make sure you're spending time learning the right things. If any of the above resonates, then this book is for you. ### What the book covers We will be covering a wide range of topics based on my own experience as a web developer. These are the things that have been important to know about in my career. It is only my own point of view and your own path will be different, but by the end you should have enough information to continue your own growth with confidence. This is not an intro to HTML and CSS nor is it a deep dive into Ruby or PHP web application coding. We will not be building another todo app or blog. This book sits somewhere in between to introduce you to a broad, but focused, range of topics you will encounter as a web developer. We'll start with getting your development environment set up and introduce you to version control with Git. From there we'll head into the development side of things with Sass, Ruby, Liquid templates, and Markdown. Throughout, we will be using Jekyll, a simple yet powerful static-site generator. Best of all, it's free during development.
